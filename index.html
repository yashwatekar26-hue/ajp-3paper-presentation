PAPER 1 · REVIEW

Overview of AJP Technology

Wilkinson et al. (2019) · A review of aerosol jet printing

⚙️ Mechanism

Atomize → Transport → Focus → Deposit

Ultrasonic (1-10 cP) or Pneumatic (1-1000 cP) atomization, carrier gas, sheath gas focusing, 1-5 mm stand-off.

📦 Materials

Silver Inks · Ceramics · DNA

From silver nanoparticles to biomolecules (proteins, DNA). Extremely wide material compatibility.

🎯 Applications

Electronics · Sensing · Bio

Passive/Active components, sensors, 3D structures, cell patterning.

⚠️ Limitations: Hard-to-control overspray · Empirical ink formulation · Resolution limited to ~10 μm.

PAPER 2 · INNOVATION

Multi-Material 3D Printing
Solving the Nanomaterial Dispersion Problem

Hwang et al. (2024) · Highly dispersed nanomaterials in polymer matrix via AM3D printing

💡 Core Idea

Atomize Separately · Mix at Nozzle

Inspired by graffiti art: Polymer and nanomaterials are atomized separately and combined only at the printhead → prevents pre-mixing agglomeration.

🔬 Key Result

Perfect Ag Nanoparticle Dispersion

SEM shows no agglomeration. Uniform dielectric constant with significantly lower variation than conventional methods.

📐 3D Capability

Multi-layer · No Cross-linkers

Stacked thermoplastic polymer layers. 16x increase in dielectric constant. Demonstrated in TENG devices.

⚠️ Limitations: Resolution remains at the micrometer scale · Only demonstrated for Ag/PI and Ag/PVDF-TrFE systems.

PAPER 3 · BREAKTHROUGH

Charged Aerosol 3D Nanoprinting

Jung et al. (2019) · Three-dimensional nanoprinting via charged aerosol focusing

⚡ Core Principle

Electrostatic Focusing Lens

Ions accumulate on the mask → create an electric field funnel → guide charged nanoparticles along field lines to the substrate.

🌀 Two Modes

Growth Mode + Writing Mode

Slow stage movement → 3D growth (pillars, helices). Fast movement → writing (lines, letters).

📏 Resolution

< 100 nm

True nanoscale 3D printing. Fabricated helices, zig-zags, stairs, and letters. Multi-material switching (Pd/Cu).

⚠️ Limitations: Only demonstrated for metal nanoparticles · No multi-material composite yet · Slow growth speed (~50 μm/h).

RESEARCH QUESTION

A Question Born from the GAP

"How can we integrate the electrostatic-guided 3D nanoprinting approach from Paper 3
with the separate-atomization multi-material strategy from Paper 2,
to achieve multi-material, true 3D nanostructures with sub-100 nm resolution in a single hybrid process?"

🎯 Why This Question Matters

  • ✓ P3 demonstrated single-material 3D nano-structures, but not multi-material.
  • ✓ P2 demonstrated multi-material composites, but at the micro-scale.
  • ✓ Combining them → A new paradigm: "Multi-material 3D Nano-printing".

🔬 Potential Applications

  • ✓ 3D Nano-electronics
  • ✓ Multi-functional Nano-sensors
  • ✓ Heterogeneous Nano-devices
